Liquid 3 is an innovative urban photobioreactor designed to combat air pollution, developed in Serbia. It uses microalgae in a 600-liter water tank to conduct photosynthesis, capturing carbon dioxide (CO2) and releasing oxygen (O2). This technology is significantly more efficient than traditional trees, absorbing up to 50 times more CO2.

Developed by Dr. Ivan Spasojević and colleagues from the University of Belgrade, Liquid 3 provides a sustainable approach to urban greening, equating to the CO2 absorption of one mature tree or 200 square meters of grass ￼ ￼. It offers multifunctionality, including ambient lighting and solar panels that charge USB devices.

LIQUID3 is urban photo-bioreactor that uses the power of microalgae to efficiently remove CO2 and produce O2 and biomass. It represents an alternative sustainable concept of greening of urban environments that show limited space and heavy pollution. LIQUID3 replaces one adult tree or 200 m2 of lawn and operates during the winter.

Unlike tree planting, which may require significant space and could disrupt local infrastructure, Liquid 3 can be installed quickly and moved as needed with minimal impact. This flexibility makes it an adaptable option for cities facing spatial and logistical challenges ￼ ￼.
